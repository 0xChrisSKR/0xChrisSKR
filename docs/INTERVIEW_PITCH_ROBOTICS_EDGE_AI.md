# Interview Pitch: Robotics / Edge AI Engineer

## 60-Second Pitch

My robotics direction is early and honest: I own Unitree GO2 Air hardware, and I am building a public research lab around ROS2 learning, AI command schemas, and future TRACE Agent integration. I do not claim completed ROS2 integration, autonomous navigation, or a shipped robotics product. What I can show today is the architecture direction: connect AI agent workflow design with physical systems through explicit command schemas, safety boundaries, validation, and demo readiness. My existing AI platform and infrastructure work gives me a foundation for edge-AI systems where reliability and reviewability matter.

## 3 Project Examples

- GO2 Agent Lab: planned public repository for Embodied AI, ROS2 learning, and future TRACE Agent integration.
- TRACE AI Platform: agent workflow and system-boundary thinking.
- Immune RPC Gate: reliability and safe-mode thinking that transfers to edge systems.

## Honest Weaknesses

- ROS2 integration is not completed yet.
- No autonomous navigation claim is made.
- I need public video evidence after the first safe GO2 demos exist.

## What I Am Learning Next

- ROS2 fundamentals.
- Ubuntu robotics development environment setup.
- GO2 connectivity and safe command boundaries.
- Function Calling / Structured Output command schemas for robot actions.

## Questions To Ask Interviewers

- How does your robotics team separate high-level planning from low-level control?
- What safety boundaries are required before AI can issue robot commands?
- How do you test edge-AI behavior before hardware execution?
- What ROS2 and deployment practices should I strengthen first?
